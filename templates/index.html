Benji Levine is an enthusiastic and hardworking recent Computer Systems Engineering graduate from the University of Warwick, with skills in software engineering, system administration, and DevOps.


Benji is currently travelling the world to learn about different cultures and discover new areas of interest.

Having started travelling in October 2019, he has so far visited Madrid and Paris.


Benji graduated from the University of Warwick in July 2019 with a high 2:1 in Computer Systems Engineering BEng. He was one of the first eight students to study Computer Systems Engineering at Warwick, and one of the first five to graduate. The course provided a variety of valuable skills, particularly helped by Benji's personal tutors; Matt Leeke and Suhaib Fahmy.

Suhaib Fahmy also supervised Benji during his final year project on Modelling Smart Field-Programmable Gate Array Switches in the Network. The report for this project can be found here, and the project code is publicly available on GitHub.


Whilst at Warwick, Benji held the position of IT Officer at Warwick Student Cinema for two years. This position involved managing all of the society's IT equipment, including firewalls, Windows and Linux servers, a large PHP-based website, various database, containers, virtual machines, an internal Wi-Fi network, network switches, an Active Directory for 50+ users, Windows workstations, an electronic point-of-sale (EPOS) system, and more. The experience gained from this position was invaluable, providing exposure to equipment and architectures which are hard to find outside of a professional environment.
